# Social Reversal Parables

Recovered structural placement:

This chapter treats the Prodigal Son, the Vineyard Workers, and Rich Man and Lazarus as parables of inversion.

Core inversions:

- Inside and outside.
- First and last.
- Rich and poor.
- Righteous and sinner.
- Obedient and restored.

Function:

These parables show that the movement’s architecture does not merely comfort outsiders. It destabilizes status systems by making inherited rank unreliable. Early identity inversion becomes late-stage sorting.
