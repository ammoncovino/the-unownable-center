# Red Words as Interface

Recovered structural placement:

This chapter shows the red sayings as a perception interface, not merely moral slogans.

Core motifs:

- Light
- Eye
- Blindness
- Hidden and revealed
- Lamp
- Whitewash
- Darkness

Recovered claims:

- The sayings privilege visibility and long-term exposure.
- They treat perception as calibratable.
- They evaluate authority by its relationship to light, not by claim.
- They form an interface that teaches listeners what to notice and what to distrust.

Bridge:

The next chapter should show how the sayings form mirror-pairs across the movement arc: early blessings and instructions returning later as final exposures and judgments.
