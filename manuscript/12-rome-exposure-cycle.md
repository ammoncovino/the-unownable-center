# Rome and the Exposure Cycle

Recovered structural placement:

Rome is read as a high-complexity system running on tribute, military projection, legal administration, spectacle, and propaganda.

Core claims:

- Empire stabilizes itself by extracting from the edges and narrating order at the center.
- Persecution and spectacle act like arena mechanisms.
- Bread-and-circuses reduce pressure without resolving the structural conditions producing pressure.
- Rome exposes the danger of a system that confuses continuity with legitimacy.

Bridge:

Rome leads naturally into arena stories because empire makes structure visible through spectacle. Modern fiction then gives readers cleaner diagrams of the same system logic.
