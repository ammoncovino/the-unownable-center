# Genesis: The First Drift

Recovered structural placement:

Genesis is read as the first drift from reality into model inflation. The knowledge of good and evil is not treated merely as rule-breaking. It is the moment a human system begins to act as if its internal model can stand over reality itself.

Core claims to develop:

- The Eden narrative can be read as a story of model inflation.
- Shame, hiding, and blame appear after the system becomes self-conscious.
- The serpent, the tree, and the act of eating can be read as a primitive architecture of desire, interpretation, choice, consequence, and displaced responsibility.
- The structural lesson is not “humans are bad.” It is that humans become dangerous when they confuse their model of reality with reality itself.

Bridge:

If Genesis shows the first drift inside the human being, Exodus shows the same drift at the level of a community trying to make the invisible center ownable.
