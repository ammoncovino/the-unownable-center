# The Lord’s Prayer as Governor

The steward thread identifies this chapter as a completed systems reading of Matthew 6:9–13 as an architecture of restraint, not a talisman.

Recovered structural notes:

- “Our Father” de-centers individual ego and invokes shared authority.
- “Thy kingdom come, thy will be done” places human projects under an external constraint and prevents totalizing self-rule.
- “Give us… forgive us… lead us not” treats humans as systems that need daily provision, debt-release loops, and guardrails against temptation and exposure cycles.

Function in the book:

This chapter is the template for later readings. It shows a sacred text functioning as a governor: a repeated form that limits what the speaker can claim, accumulate, or demand. Later chapters look for similar governors in civic, imperial, platform, and AI systems.
