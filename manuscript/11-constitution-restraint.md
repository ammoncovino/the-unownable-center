# The Constitution as Restraint Architecture

Recovered structural placement:

The Constitution is read as a secular governor.

Core claims:

- Separation of powers functions like “lead us not into temptation” for government.
- Enumerated rights operate as non-negotiable constraints on majority moods.
- Checks and balances assume that power drifts and must be structurally limited.
- Constitutional drift mirrors scriptural drift: texts are preserved while interpretation, incentives, and institutional survival pressures change the system’s behavior.

Function:

This chapter moves the book from sacred governors to civic governors. The same structural question remains: can power be designed so that it cannot easily claim the center for itself?
